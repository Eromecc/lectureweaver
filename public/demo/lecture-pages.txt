STRATEGY 01
Retrieval beats passive review
LECTUREWEAVER SYNTHETIC DEMO RETRIEVAL PRACTICE 02 / 08
Close the material. Pull the answer from memory. Then check.
WHY IT WORKS
Strengthens access to knowledge
and exposes gaps that
familiarity can hide.
WHAT COUNTS
Low-stakes quizzes, blank-page
recall, flashcards, and
explaining without slides.
WHAT DOES NOT
Viewing the answer immediately
and recognizing it is not
retrieval practice.
LEARN > RETRIEVE > CHECK > RESTUDY THE GAP

STRATEGY 02
Space practice, then schedule it
LECTUREWEAVER SYNTHETIC DEMO SPACING + SCHEDULES 03 / 08
Spacing distributes practice across separate sessions. A practice schedule turns that
principle into a repeatable plan.
DAY 0

DAY 3
Retrieve
DAY 7

DAY 14

SPACING EFFECT
Some forgetting makes the next successful
retrieval effortful and useful.
ADAPT THE INTERVAL
After a failure, return sooner. After easy recall,
lengthen the interval.

--- LectureWeaver demo page 4 ---
DESIGN PRINCIPLE
Difficulty is useful only when it teaches
LECTUREWEAVER SYNTHETIC DEMO DESIRABLE DIFFICULTIES 04 / 08
DESIRABLE DIFFICULTY Harder now, better retention or transfer later.
PRODUCTIVE FRICTION
Generation before explanation
Spaced retrieval and varied examples
Achievable effort with correction
MERELY DIFFICULT
Confusing or ambiguous instructions
A task beyond current prerequisites
Effort with no useful feedback

THE MECHANISM: DISCRIMINATION
Notice features > Identify the problem > Choose a strategy
Interleaving can lower immediate accuracy while improving later strategy selection and transfer. Smooth
blocked practice may be misleading.

--- LectureWeaver demo page 6 ---
CORRECTION LOOP
Feedback timing closes the loop
LECTUREWEAVER SYNTHETIC DEMO FEEDBACK 06 / 08
Give feedback after a genuine attempt, but soon enough to correct the mental model before an
error becomes stable.
1 ATTEMPT 2 FEEDBACK 3 CORRECTION 4 RETRY
USE SOONER
Novices, factual errors, and misconceptions that
could persist.
A BRIEF DELAY CAN HELP
Complex judgments where reflection is useful -
never leave errors uncorrected.

--- LectureWeaver demo page 7 ---
REALITY CHECK
Fluency is not mastery
LECTUREWEAVER SYNTHETIC DEMO REREADING 07 / 08
FEELS EASY
Recognition
The page looks familiar. Each pass is
faster. Highlighted sentences feel known.
Rereading alone: low utility
BUILDS DURABLE ACCESS
Retrieval
The material is closed. You produce the
idea, check it, and repair a specific gap.
Practice testing: stronger evidence
Use rereading for orientation or targeted restudy - not as proof that learning happened.
